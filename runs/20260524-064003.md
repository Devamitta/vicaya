## What surprised me
- The CST English database is internally inconsistent: uses "existence" for three-realm classification (SN45.164) and "becoming" for PS chain (Vibhanga), even within the same tool run.
- Bhikkhu Brahmali's argument against "becoming" is the strongest I've seen: bhavataṇhā = "craving for becoming" = craving for change, which makes no psychological sense — we crave stable states.
- SuttaCentral forum thread "Bhava doesn't mean becoming" (2022) is a high-value scholarly source that was easy to miss — worth bookmarking as a standing reference.
