## What surprised me
- Buddhaghosa's Sāratthappakāsinī glosses bhavanirodho as pañcakkhandhanirodho — the strongest cessationist reading comes from the commentary, not the mūla
- The mūla's bhava definition is always restricted to three conditioned realms + kamma-process; extension to pañcakkhandha is a commentarial move
- SN 12.68 (Nārada passage): a non-returner directly knows bhavanirodho nibbāna — so the phrase refers to an insight-object accessible before arahantship, not only a post-mortem event
