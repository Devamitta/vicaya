## What surprised me
- SN35.96 uses "cha abhibhāyatanāni" (SIX bases of overcoming) for sense-restraint at the six sense-doors — a doctrinally distinct meaning of the same compound, easily missed.
- Mahāprajñāpāramitāśāstra makes a sharp three-way distinction (vimokṣa / abhibhāyatana / kṛtsnāyatana) that the Theravāda commentaries collapse into "different names for the same jhāna" — major cross-school doctrinal divergence.
- MA 215 (Madhyamāgama parallel to AN10.29) adds an explicit appamāṇa-citta gloss per colour-abhibhāyatana that the Pāḷi entirely lacks.
