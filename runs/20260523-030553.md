## What surprised me
- The Kaṇṭakasutta (AN 10.72) "thorn" argument is a genuinely strong canonical counter to the literalist position, not just a modern rationalist objection
- The Visuddhimagga §229 passage is *not* the classic "four who do not breathe" list — it lists seven, and occurs in the context of diagnosing why a meditator *has lost* their breath object (i.e., it reassures them they are not in the fourth jhāna)
- Polak's argument about the Jain breathless-meditation-as-dying is stronger than expected: the Uttarajjhayana Sutta passage is about conscious dying, not a recoverable trance
