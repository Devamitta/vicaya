## What surprised me
- MN127 Anuruddhasuttaṃ contains a definitive, explicit canonical answer: the householder *specifically asks* whether appamāṇā and mahaggatā cetovimutti are the same, and the Buddha explicitly says they differ in meaning AND phrasing. This is the cleanest canonical resolution I've seen on a semantic question.
- The Dhammasaṅgaṇī fourfold matrix (appamāṇa jhāna with parittārammaṇa = immeasurable jhāna with limited object) is an elegant Abhidhamma confirmation that appamāṇa is a jhāna quality independent of its object's spatial extent.
