## What surprised me
- The Vibhaṅga contains both definitions explicitly side by side — the suttanta-bhājanīya uses cetanā exclusively (§§20–25) while the abhidhamma-bhājanīya uses cittasampayutto (§92). This internal dual structure is the clearest possible evidence that the Abhidhamma tradition *knew* about both approaches and deliberately maintained them as complementary methods, not replacements.
- Even within the abhidhamma-bhājanīya, when the Vibhaṅga breaks down saṅkhārakkhandha into a sixfold subdivision (§300), it reverts to cetanā language — showing cetanā remains the organizational anchor even in the broader analysis.
- Bhikkhu Bodhi, despite being the foremost modern voice for the Abhidhamma tradition, substantially agrees that in the khandha and paṭicca contexts, saṅkhārā = volitions. His position is more nuanced than his Comprehensive Manual suggests.
- Dhammasaṅgaṇī §62 puts phassa (contact) first in the saṅkhārakkhandha list — before cetanā — which is often overlooked in presentations of the "50 cetasikā" formula.
