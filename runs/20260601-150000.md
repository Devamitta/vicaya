## What surprised me
- The MN44 anusaya passage contradicts Arbel's account comprehensively — she claims the second and third jhānas abandon aversion, but the text shows aversion is abandoned through reflective longing for liberation, not through those jhānas. This is not interpretation; it is a factual misreading.
- Both scholars cited the same sutta passages and drew opposite conclusions — the canon search confirmed Anālayo's readings in every single case.
- Arbel's Chapter 1 arguing the fourfold model is a Buddhist innovation is her strongest section but was barely addressed by Anālayo — he focused on individual text readings.

## What I'd change in the skill
- The Calibre lock detection needs a non-blocking probe — both searches timed out at 120s waiting on the lock, and the fallback was a direct `find` on disk. A quick `fuser` or lock-file timestamp check before running `calibredb` would prevent the timeout.
- For library-heavy research questions where the user names specific books, a "named-book extraction" subcommand that takes a filename/author and returns the path and pdftotext output directly (bypassing calibredb) would save both timeout delays and lock contention.
