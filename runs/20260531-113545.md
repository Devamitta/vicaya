## What surprised me
- The Mūlasarvāstivāda and Sanskrit parallel traditions give a completely different reading (saṃghāvaśeṣa = "remainder in the Saṅgha") that emphasizes the monk STILL HAS STANDING, contrasting with pārājika — this is a distinct angle that enriches the translation discussion beyond the Theravāda etymological reading.
- Horner explicitly acknowledges the controversy in her introduction: "Like the meaning of pārājika, the meaning of saṅghādisesa is controversial" — yet still chose "suspension" in rule headings.
- Brahmali leaves the term untranslated across all his Vinaya work — this may now be the default in serious Vinaya communities.
